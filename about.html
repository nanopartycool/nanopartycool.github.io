Why I Do This

I have overcome paralysis, physical (dis)ability and other adversity. Facing these challenges, I have found that my passion was the key to success. Following my passion for microbiology led me to wanting to change the status quo and seek a means for better treatments, better diagnostics and greater access to care. That's why I became a bioengineer!

Research Interests

Serological assay development, NAAT development

Assay automation, Microfluidic applications, Image analysis

3-D printing, Custom-fit prosthetic design

Other Things I Do

Ceramics: {Hand building, wheel-throwing, and clay and glaze making; I Enjoy Raku and high-fire pottery. Ceramics has always been an artistic outlet for me, and enjoy spending long hours in the studio on the wheel and using my chemistry knowledge to make new glazes.

3-D Printing: CAD hobbyist and making my own functional and aesthetic designs which I am able to 3-D print, and enjoy assisting troubleshooting 3-D printing especially for bioengineering and wet-lab applications. I have previously assisted teams involved with tissue printing and creation of bioreactors for cell culture.

Traveling: Having a brother live 12 time zones away in Thailand, I have had the opportunity to travel around the world. As a young boy I was fascinated by cartography, and where the most remote and pristine places are to find. I enjoy traveling and experiencing new places while showing my gratefulness through service.
